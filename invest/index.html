WHY INVEST IN BALI?

More 6 million international tourists and 7 million domestic tourists travel to Bali every year. Ignoring the shocking effects of the pandemic for a minute, this has created a thriving tourism industry that has grown steadily since the 90’s, now accounting for about 80% of the island’s economy.

In addition to tourism, a more flexible global workforce has resulted in many location-agnostic workers with high net worths re-locating to Bali. As the island’s expat community continues to grow rapidly, real estate transactions in the luxury segment follow suit, with an increasing share of foreign investment in the past several years.

With favorable local laws and regulations for investors, Bali’s property sector is a fantastic investment option for both business and personal purposes.

Bali’s reputation as a budget-friendly, luxury travel destination continues to gain international exposure, with millions of tourists making this island their favourite. From stunning beaches, lush greenery and magical temples to an extraordinary varied culinary scene and a vibrant nightlife, there is something for everyone.

TOURISM ECONOMY

The tourism sector makes up around 80% of Bali’s economy. In 2019, more than 6 million international tourists visited Bali which accounted for 40% of all tourism in Indonesia.

HISTORY

Bali's history is deep, ranging from Majahapit Empire to Dutch colonialism. People have inhabited the island since 2000 BC, but the first European contact was made in the 16th century.

CLIMATE

The climate in Bali is tropical. Located 8 degrees south of the equator, Bali has two seasons: the dry season, from April to October and the wet season, from November to March.

RELIGION

Although Indonesia is primarily a Muslim country, the main religion in Bali is Hindu. Christian and Muslim religions do exist, but Hinduism covers around 84% of the population.
